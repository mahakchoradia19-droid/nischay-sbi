# Field Kit — the 3-day plan to make this un-ignorable

The single biggest upgrade available to this submission is not in the code. It is
**evidence that already happened**: a handful of real people, one real video, three
numbers of your own. This kit is everything needed to collect that in 3 days.

Why it matters: judges read hundreds of "AI for banking" documents. Almost none of
them contain a sentence like *"We asked 40 real people; 68% said X"* or a video of a
real person's face when the money arrives. Primary evidence is what separates a
proposal from a proof.

---

## Part 1 — The micro-survey (launch tonight, WhatsApp)

**Target:** 30–50 responses. Family and friends in tier-2/3 towns, shopkeepers,
anyone who holds a Jan Dhan / basic account or receives a scheme benefit
(PM-KISAN, MGNREGS, scholarship, pension, LPG subsidy). Send in Hindi first;
English below for reference. Google Forms or a plain WhatsApp message both work —
what matters is that the answers are real and you can quote them.

**Intro (Hindi):**
"नमस्ते! मैं एक स्टूडेंट प्रोजेक्ट के लिए 2 मिनट का छोटा सर्वे कर रही हूँ — सरकारी पैसा
(जैसे PM-Kisan, पेंशन, वज़ीफ़ा) बैंक खाते तक पहुँचने के बारे में। कोई भी जवाब गलत नहीं
है। आपका नाम कहीं इस्तेमाल नहीं होगा।"

**The questions:**

1. क्या आपको या आपके परिवार में किसी को सरकारी योजना का पैसा मिलता है? (PM-Kisan /
   मनरेगा / पेंशन / वज़ीफ़ा / गैस सब्सिडी / अन्य / नहीं)
   *(Does anyone in your family receive government scheme money?)*
2. क्या कभी ऐसा हुआ कि पैसा आना चाहिए था लेकिन खाते में नहीं आया? (हाँ, कई बार / हाँ,
   एक-दो बार / नहीं / पता नहीं)
   *(Has expected money ever not arrived?)* ← **your headline stat**
3. अगर नहीं आया, तो आपको कैसे पता चला? (बैंक जाकर / किसी ने बताया / कभी पता ही नहीं
   चला / और कुछ)
   *(How did you find out — or did you never find out?)* ← **the "silent failure" stat**
4. क्या आपका या घर में किसी का खाता कभी "बंद/निष्क्रिय" हुआ है या KYC के लिए बैंक
   बुलाया गया है? (हाँ / नहीं / पता नहीं)
5. बैंक का काम ठीक कराने के लिए ब्रांच जाने में क्या सबसे मुश्किल है? (दूरी / छुट्टी लेनी
   पड़ती है / लाइन/समय / समझ नहीं आता क्या कागज़ चाहिए / कुछ मुश्किल नहीं)
6. क्या आप फ़ोन पर हिंदी में बात करके बैंक का काम (जैसे KYC) पूरा करना पसंद करेंगे,
   बजाय ब्रांच जाने के? (हाँ, ज़रूर / शायद / नहीं, ब्रांच ही ठीक है)
7. फ़ोन पर बैंक का काम करने में सबसे बड़ा डर क्या है? (गलती हो जाने का डर / धोखाधड़ी
   का डर / समझ नहीं आएगा / कोई डर नहीं) ← **the fear stat**
8. आपकी उम्र? (18–30 / 31–45 / 46–60 / 60+)
9. आप कहाँ रहते हैं? (गाँव / छोटा शहर / बड़ा शहर)
10. कुछ और बताना चाहें? (खुला जवाब — **verbatim quotes come from here**)
11. क्या आप 5 मिनट का एक छोटा डेमो आज़माकर राय देना चाहेंगे? हाँ तो अपना WhatsApp
    नंबर लिखें। *(This recruits your Day-2 test users.)*

**How to use the results:** pick the 3 strongest numbers (Q2, Q3, Q7) + 1 verbatim
quote from Q10, and put them on the first page of the proposal and the top of the
README. Example of the sentence you're trying to earn:
*"Of 40 people we surveyed, 14 had personally had scheme money fail to arrive — and
9 of them only found out at the branch, weeks later."*

## Part 2 — The 90-second demo video (film on Day 2)

**Who:** one real person — a parent, an aunt, a shopkeeper. Someone who would
plausibly receive a scheme payment, speaking their real language. Get spoken
consent on camera ("क्या मैं यह वीडियो प्रोजेक्ट में दिखा सकती हूँ?").

**Where:** their real environment — the shop counter, the kitchen, the courtyard.
Ambient noise is *good*: it proves this isn't a studio trick.

**The script (shot by shot):**

| Time | Shot | What happens |
|---|---|---|
| 0–10s | Their face, talking to camera | One line, in their words: "पैसा आना था, आया नहीं। पता भी नहीं चला।" |
| 10–20s | Over the shoulder: the phone | The SMS arrives: *"आपकी सरकारी राशि अटक सकती है — बात करके ठीक करें →"* They tap it. |
| 20–60s | Split: their face + the screen | The agent greets them **in Hindi, out loud**. It explains the problem. It asks the one confirming question. **They answer by speaking.** Camera stays on the face. |
| 60–75s | The screen | The gate passes. "खाता सक्रिय · ₹2,000 आ गए." The chime. |
| 75–90s | Their face | Their real reaction. Then one closing line on black: **"No form. No branch. No queue. The money arrived."** |

**The one rule:** the camera is on the *face* at the confirmation moment, not the
screen. The product is the screen; the proof is the face.

**Failure playbook (rehearse before filming):**
- Rehearse the confirm phrase ("हाँ, एक ही") with the speaker 10+ times — speech
  recognition on-device varies with dialect and noise.
- If the mic mis-hears twice, the buttons are the designed fallback — *use them on
  camera and say so out loud*: "and when the mic can't hear, it never guesses — it
  asks you to tap." That turns a failure into a safety feature.
- Film 3 full takes. Keep the best.
- For any live demo later: pre-load the video on the laptop **and** a USB stick;
  never depend on venue wifi; if a live run mis-fires once, switch to the video
  without apologising.

## Part 3 — Where the evidence goes

1. **Proposal, page 1:** your 3 survey numbers + 1 quote, in a short box titled
   "What real people told us."
2. **README, top section:** the same numbers, one line, linked to the video.
3. **A "what broke in the field" paragraph** — write down exactly what failed in
   testing (mis-heard words, confusion at a step, a person who didn't trust the
   SMS). Reporting your own failures is the most credible sentence in any
   submission, and it matches how the rest of this project already talks.
4. **The video:** upload unlisted (YouTube/Drive), link it in the README and the
   proposal, and keep the offline copy for demo day.

**Final pre-submission sanity check:**
- [ ] Survey numbers appear in the first page of the proposal
- [ ] Video plays offline from the local file
- [ ] Every claim in the doc is cited, computed, or came from your field test
- [ ] No stale names anywhere (`grep -ri "saarthi\|yono" .`)
- [ ] `python3 test_engine.py` passes
- [ ] Submit hours early, never at the deadline
